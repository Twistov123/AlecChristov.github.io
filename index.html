Metal Melting Transformer

DIY High-Power Project

Project Overview

A high-power project involving the repurposing of microwave oven transformers (MOTs) to create a metal melting rig capable of melting small quantities of metal through resistive heating.

Key Achievements

  • Successfully rewound microwave transformer secondary for high-current, low-voltage output
  • Designed safe switching and control circuitry for high-power operation
  • Implemented thermal management solutions for sustained operation
  • Achieved sufficient power output to melt aluminium and other metals

Skills Developed

  • Transformer theory and practical rewinding techniques
  • High-current circuit design and safety considerations
  • Thermal management for high-power applications
  • Safe handling of dangerous voltages and currents

Technical Details

The project involved removing the original secondary winding from MOTs and replacing it with a few turns of heavy gauge cable to achieve very high currents at low voltage. This required understanding of transformer principles, wire gauge selection for current capacity, and careful attention to electrical safety.

Dual-Resonance Tesla Coil

Steady-State Design

Project Overview

An ongoing project to design and build a dual-resonance steady-state Tesla coil (DRSSTC). This project explores high-voltage RF design, resonant circuit theory, and power electronics at the advanced hobbyist level.

Current Progress

  • Researching DRSSTC topology and control strategies
  • Designing primary and secondary coil geometries
  • Selecting components for the H-bridge driver
  • Planning feedback and interrupter circuits

Skills Being Developed

  • High-voltage design and safety
  • RF and resonant circuit theory
  • H-bridge and gate driver design
  • Coil winding and tuning techniques
  • EMI considerations and mitigation

Technical Approach

The DRSSTC approach uses solid-state switching (IGBTs or MOSFETs) to drive the primary coil at its resonant frequency, with feedback from the secondary ensuring the system stays in tune. This allows for controlled, repeatable operation compared to spark-gap designs.

DIY Wind Turbine

Renewable Energy Exploration

⚠ Project Discontinued

This project was discontinued after prototyping revealed fundamental feasibility issues. While unsuccessful, it provided valuable lessons in renewable energy system design and the importance of early-stage feasibility analysis.

Project Concept

The goal was to explore small-scale renewable energy generation by repurposing an electric bike hub motor as a wind turbine generator. Hub motors can function as generators when mechanically driven, making them an attractive option for DIY wind power projects.

Approach

  • Repurposed an electric bike hub motor to act as a generator
  • Designed and fabricated turbine blades
  • Built an aluminium frame to mount the assembly
  • Planned rectification and charging circuitry for battery storage

Why It Failed

The aluminium frame and hub motor assembly proved too heavy for practical wind turbine use. The high rotational mass required wind speeds that were rarely available at ground level, and the structural requirements for supporting the weight made the project impractical for a small-scale installation.

Lessons Learned

  • Weight matters: Wind turbine design requires careful attention to rotational mass and cut-in wind speed
  • Feasibility first: Should have calculated power output vs. weight ratio before building
  • Hub motors aren't ideal: Purpose-built generators or lighter alternator designs are better suited for wind applications
  • Site assessment: Average wind speed at the installation site is critical to evaluate early

Skills Developed

  • Understanding of generator/motor duality
  • Renewable energy system fundamentals
  • Mechanical fabrication with aluminium
  • Project feasibility analysis (learned the hard way)
  • Knowing when to discontinue a project

Takeaway

Not every project succeeds, but every project teaches something. This experience reinforced the importance of feasibility analysis and calculating key parameters before committing to a full build. The lessons learned here have informed my approach to subsequent projects.
